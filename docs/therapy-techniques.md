# Common Therapy Techniques

## Evidence-Based Therapeutic Approaches

### Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is one of the most widely researched and effective forms of psychotherapy. It works on the principle that our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected.

**Key Components:**
- Identifying negative thought patterns
- Challenging and reframing unhelpful thoughts
- Developing healthier coping strategies
- Homework assignments and practice

**Best For:**
- Depression
- Anxiety disorders
- PTSD
- OCD
- Eating disorders

### Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT combines CBT techniques with mindfulness practices. Originally developed for borderline personality disorder, it's now used for various conditions.

**Core Skills:**
- Mindfulness: Being present in the moment
- Distress tolerance: Managing crises without making things worse
- Emotion regulation: Understanding and managing intense emotions
- Interpersonal effectiveness: Maintaining relationships while respecting yourself

### Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT focuses on accepting what's out of your control and committing to actions that enrich your life.

**Key Principles:**
- Psychological flexibility
- Accepting thoughts and feelings
- Being present
- Defining personal values
- Taking committed action

### Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)

EMDR is primarily used for trauma and PTSD. It involves processing traumatic memories while performing bilateral stimulation (usually eye movements).

**Process:**
1. History taking and treatment planning
2. Preparation and establishing trust
3. Assessment of target memory
4. Desensitization using bilateral stimulation
5. Installation of positive beliefs
6. Body scan
7. Closure
8. Reevaluation

### Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)

IPT focuses on improving interpersonal relationships and social functioning to help reduce psychological distress.

**Focus Areas:**
- Grief and loss
- Role transitions
- Interpersonal disputes
- Interpersonal deficits

## Therapeutic Techniques

### Exposure Therapy
Gradually exposing clients to feared situations or objects in a safe environment to reduce anxiety.

### Behavioral Activation
Scheduling activities that bring pleasure or achievement to combat depression.

### Cognitive Restructuring
Identifying and challenging negative automatic thoughts and cognitive distortions.

### Grounding Techniques
Methods to help individuals stay present and connected to reality, especially useful for anxiety and dissociation.

### Journaling
Writing down thoughts and feelings to process emotions and gain insights.
